Detailed Description

These functions allow the manipulation of various operational parameters of a Nüvü Cameras device via a NcCam handle.

Devices provided by Nüvü Camēras with pre-programmed readout modes, such as the HNü or EMN2 models of camera, will support the use of NcCam as the control context and it is recommended for most use cases.

Enumeration Type Documentation

enum Features

Values identifying features of the camera that may be available.

Availability is reported by the functions ncCamParamAvailable() or ncGrabParamAvailable() and may ultimately depend upon the specific readout mode loaded, the specific model or options included. The availability of many of these features is associated with the use of particular API functions: where appropriate, such functions are listed below with the enum values.

Note
Certain Features may be available in principle but their access restricted according to other operational parameters: e.g. CALIBRATED_EM_GAIN will only be available for readout modes having the parameter Ampli with value EM (see ncCamGetAmpliTypeAvail() and ncCamGetReadoutMode()), but in addition can only be used when the sensor temperature range is within that given by ncCamGetCalibratedEmGainTempRange().
Enumerator
UNKNOWN_FEATURE 

Generic unsupported value.

EXPOSURE 

Control of exposure time; use of ncCamSetExposureTime().

WAITING_TIME 

Control of blanking/waiting time; use of ncCamSetWaitingTime().

TRIGGER_MODE 

Also specify a particular TriggerMode as the setting, or use directly one of the TRIGGER_MODE_* values below; use of ncCamSetTriggerMode(). TDI and FKM readout can affect TriggerMode availability.

SHUTTER_MODE 

Also specify a particular ShutterMode as the setting; use of ncCamSetShutterMode(). FKM readout can affect ShutterMode availability.

SHUTTER_POLARITY 

Control of SHUTTER external signal polarity; use of ncCamSetShutterPolarity().

EXTERNAL_SHUTTER 

Independent control of an external shutter via the SHUTTER external signal; use of ncCamSetExternalShutter().

EXTERNAL_SHUTTER_MODE 

Independent control of the external shutter mode; use of ncCamSetExternalShutterMode().

EXTERNAL_SHUTTER_DELAY 

Independent control of the external shutter signal delay relative to the start of exposure (FIRE); use of ncCamSetExternalShutterDelay().

FIRE_POLARITY 

Control of FIRE external signal polarity; use of ncCamSetFirePolarity().

MINIMUM_PULSE_WIDTH 

Control over the minimum pulse width of external signals output by the camera (ARM, SHUTTER, FIRE); use of ncCamSetOutputMinimumPulseWidth().

ARM_POLARITY 

Control of ARM external signal polarity; use of ncCamSetArmPolarity().

CALIBRATED_EM_GAIN 

Calibrated Electron Multiplication gain; use of ncCamSetCalibratedEmGain(). Also depends upon sensor temperature (see ncCamGetCalibratedEmGainTempRange()).

RAW_EM_GAIN 

Direct control of the voltage bias controlling Electron Multiplication; use of ncCamSetRawEmGain(), however CALIBRATED_EM_GAIN is preferred, if available.

ANALOG_GAIN 

Control of analog gain (scaling of dynamic range); use of ncCamSetAnalogGain().

ANALOG_OFFSET 

Control of analog offset (baseline level of raw images); use of ncCamSetAnalogOffset().

TARGET_DETECTOR_TEMP 

Control of sensor temperature set-point; use of ncCamSetTargetDetectorTemp().

DETECTOR_TEMP 

Access to current sensor temperature; use of ncCamGetDetectorTemp().

CTRL_TIMESTAMP 

Control of returning timestamped images; use of ncCamSetTimestampMode().

GPS_PRESENT 

Detection of an external GPS clock.

GPS_LOCKED 

Access to GPS synchronisation of image timestamps; use of ncCamSetTimestampMode() with TimestampMode GPS_TIMESTAMP.

BINNING_X 

Also specify a particular horizontal binning setting (1, 2, 4, 8 or 16); use of ncCamSetBinningMode() with the appropriate setting. Particular ROI configurations may limit availability.

BINNING_Y 

A specific vertical binning setting may be provided or 0 to query general availability; use of ncCamSetBinningMode(). Particular ROI configurations may limit availability.

ROI 

Control over deliberate restriction of the readout region of the sensor; use of ncCamSetMRoiSize() and/or ncCamSetMRoiPosition(), etc. with index 0.

CROP_MODE_X 

Selection of horizontally-compressed, accelerated readout; use of ncCamSetCropMode() with CropMode CROP_MODE_ENABLE_X.

CROP_MODE_Y 

Selection of vertically-compressed, accelerated readout; use of ncCamSetCropMode() with CropMode CROP_MODE_ENABLE_Y.

ACTIVE_REGION 

Default readout and ROI definitions are contained within the illuminated portion of the sensor.

MULTIPLE_ROI 

Control over multiple Regions Of Interest simultaneously; use of ncCamSetMRoiSize() and/or ncCamSetMRoiPosition(), etc. with multiple indices.

TRIGGER_MODE_INTERNAL 

Selection of internally triggered readout; use of ncCamSetTriggerMode() with TriggerMode INTERNAL.

TRIGGER_MODE_EXTERNAL 

Selection of externally triggered readout; use of ncCamSetTriggerMode() with TriggerMode EXT_LOW_HIGH or EXT_HIGH_LOW.

TRIGGER_MODE_EXPOSURE 

Selection of externally triggered readout, with exposure controlled by trigger signal width; use of ncCamSetTriggerMode() with TriggerMode EXT_LOW_HIGH_EXP or EXT_HIGH_LOW_EXP.

TRIGGER_MODE_CONTINUOUS 

Selection of externally triggered readout, with no blanking/waiting between exposures; use of ncCamSetTriggerMode() with TriggerMode CONT_LOW_HIGH or CONT_HIGH_LOW.

SYSTEM_STATUS 

Access to system status polling callbacks; use of ncCamSetOnStatusUpdateCallback() and ncCamSetOnStatusAlertCallback().

SHORT_SSVA_CMD 

Internal.

BIN_CDS 

Internal.

SEQUENCE_REGISTERS 

Internal.

TDI_HEIGHT_CTRL 

TDI readout has configurable frame height (via ROI).

NC_PROC_SUPPORT_RAW 

Selection of no image-processing (i.e. raw images); use of ncCamSetProcType() or ncGrabSetProcType() with ProcType NO_PROC.

NC_PROC_SUPPORT_LM 

Selection of bias subtraction image-processing; use of ncCamSetProcType() or ncGrabSetProcType() with ProcType LM. Requires a compatible readout mode having overscan (see ncCamGetOverscanLines()).

NC_PROC_SUPPORT_PC 

Selection of photon-counting image-processing; use of ncCamSetProcType() or ncGrabSetProcType() with ProcType PC. Requires a compatible readout mode having overscan (see ncCamGetOverscanLines()).

FAST_KINETICS_MODE 

Selection of Fast Kinetics Mode readout; use of ncCamSetFastKineticsMode(). Requires a compatible readout mode.

Sets the trigger mode on the camera.

The camera trigger controls whether or not an external signal is required to initiate an acquisition and how that signal controls the acquisition. For certain modes, it is possible to set the number of images to acquire for each incoming trigger. Be sure to set the image timeout to account for the expected delay between triggers or to -1 to disable image timeouts.

Note
EXT_HIGH_LOW_EXP and EXT_LOW_HIGH_EXP are only supported by HNu cameras. CONT_HIGH_LOW and CONT_LOW_HIGH are only supported by HNu cameras with firmware version 3.1.13 or higher.
For CONT_HIGH_LOW and CONT_LOW_HIGH, the first trigger signal begins exposure of the first image and all subsequent triggers arriving more than the readout time (see documentation for ncCamGetReadoutTime()) since the previous trigger cause a new frame to be read out.
The operation of some trigger modes may be affected if frame latency has been introduced by your configuration of the camera; refer to the documentation for ncCamGetFrameLatency().
Parameters

triggerModeThis parameter represents the trigger mode that will be set on the camera. To set the trigger mode:
  • Use "-3" or "CONT_HIGH_LOW" to trigger on a high to low transition in continuous acquisition mode, whereby there is no waiting time between images and each trigger after the first causes a single frame to be read out;
  • Use "-2" or "EXT_HIGH_LOW_EXP" to trigger the start of each exposure on the high to low transition and have the exposure time dictated by the trigger-low duration;

Sets the binning modes of the camera.

The binning parameters offer the possibility to group several pixels into one directly on the output register of the sensor. In doing so, a single pixel corresponds to the summation of all the pixels it regroups. As such, binning is a technique that may offer higher frame-rates by combinning several pixels into one, although this inherently reduces spatial resolution as a trade-off. Binning can be done horizontally and/or vertically (in the X- and Y-directions, respectively). When pixels are binned together vertically, the frame-rate increases proportionally: e.g. for three pixels combined into one, the frame-rate approximately triples, and so on. When pixels are binned together horizontally, any increase in frame-rate depends upon the horizontal readout frequency; for fast readout modes there is no increase.

Also note that, when using EM-gain, vertical binning occurs before EM multiplication while horizontal binning occurs afterwards.

In general horizontal binning is restricted to the values 1, 2, 4, 8 or 16; vertical binning, if available, may have any value.

int ncCamSetCropMode ( NcCam  cam,
enum CropMode  mode,
int  paddingPixelsMinimumX,

Manipulates the state of crop-mode which can accelerate the frame-rate when reading only a small ROI. This feature should only be enabled when a restricted portion of the detector receives light.

The illuminated portion usually results from the usage of a mask that is preventing the light from reaching the entire detector. When enabled, crop-mode accelerates the readout of a ROI when possible. To proceed to such an acceleration, the readout sequence is compressed. A consequence of compressing the readout sequence is that illuminated areas beyond the ROI may be superimposed onto other parts of the image. To avoid getting corrupted images because of this superimposition, the ROI should be set equal in size or larger than the illuminated portion of the image detector. Also, a minimum padding within the compressed readout sequence can be applied to try and avoid the superimposition. Any padding will be removed before an image is returned to the user. The least padding that may be applied is one pixel, which may leave no margin of error for the definition of the ROI. On the other hand, if the paddingPixelsMinimum parameter is set larger than that it may preclude the fastest readout possible, depending upon the exact size and position of the ROI. The usage of the different NcCropModeSolutions functions allows the retrieval of the different options and their performance. If a negative value is supplied for the paddingPixelsMinimumX parameter, the quantity of padding will be increased automatically to ensure a uniform level of background signal within each line. If you do not wish to alter the current minimum padding, supply the value 0. If the crop-mode was disabled and then reenabled, the usage of 0 will retrieve the previous paddingPixelsMinimum parameter used. When crop-mode is disabled, the camera returns to standard ROI readout and the paddingPixelsMinimum parameters are ignored.

Note
This function cannot be executed while an acquisition is in progress (or in queue). To do so, the ncCamAbort() function can be used to ensure that there are no pending acquisitions. As a result the acquisition queue is cleared meaning that you cannot retrieve the images previously acquired. The counts of missed images, overruns and timeouts are reset.
The ROI corresponding to the illuminated part of the detector can be set using the ncCamSetRoi() or ncCamSetMRoiSize() functions. If the crop-mode is enabled and the ROI is modified using the ncCamSetRoi() or ncCamSetMRoiSize() functions, the crop-mode readout will be recalculated automatically and will remain enabled.
ncCamMoveRoi() will not be operational when crop-mode is enabled and will return en error if it is called. An alternative is to use ncCamSetMRoiPosition() in combination with ncCamMRoiCanApplyWithoutStop() to verify if moving the ROI will require an on-going acquisition to be aborted before ncCamMRoiApply() is used to commit to using the new ROI position.
This functionality is not presently compatible with the simultaneous use of multiple ROIs. If you attempt to enable crop-mode while multiple ROIs have been defined, this function will return an error. If you attempt to add and apply additional ROIs (via ncCamAddMRoi() and ncCamMRoiApply()) while crop-mode is enabled, the requested ROIs will be put in place but crop-mode will be suspended until they are deleted and only a single ROI has been applied.
The various available types of crop-mode have differing advantages and limitations. CROP_MODE_ENABLE_XY (or CROP_MODE_ENABLE_MX) will provide the fastest frame-rate possible but will generate a delay in the arrival of images (see documentation for ncCamGetFrameLatency()) and may restrict the ability to move the ROI without aborting an on-going acquisition. CROP_MODE_ENABLE_X (or CROP_MODE_ENABLE_ZL) provides most of the acceleration available with crop-mode, especially for ROIs with a small width, and without incurring a delay in the arrival of images. CROP_MODE_ENABLE_Y (or CROP_MODE_ENABLE_SP) provides the acceleration available with crop-mode for ROIs with small height along with stable frame-rate regardless of the horizontal position of the ROI, but will generate a delay in the arrival of images (see documentation for ncCamGetFrameLatency()) .
This functionality is mutually exclusive with Fast Kinetics Mode. Crop-mode cannot be enabled while Fast Kinetics Mode is enabled.

Sets the timestamp mode of the camera.

The timestamp mode specifies if the camera supplies an image timestamp and, if so, how the camera's time is synchronised. The timestamp is the actual time that the camera sends the image to the host (at the exact end of the exposure) and may be retrieved for a specific image using the function ncCamGetCtrlTimestamp. To retrieve a valid timestamp, you must first use this function to select a TimestampMode other than NO_TIMESTAMP. The camera's time can be synchronized with a GPS timing module (if connected to the camera and with a valid signal received) by selecting the TimestampMode GPS_TIMESTAMP. The internal camera timestamp can also be synchronised by using the ncCamSetTimestampInternal function.

Note

To do so, the ncCamAbort function can be used to interrupt the acquisition queue and ensure that there are no pending acquisitions. The acquisition queue is emptied (you cannot retrieve the images previously acquired). The counts of missed images, the overrun and timeout flags are reset.
